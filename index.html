Social Impact

Every year, 1.3 billion tons of food is lost or wasted. We are currently facing a severe food shortage created by the global food system. But the negative effects extend to areas above that, including climate change, deforestation, pollution, loss of soil, loss of biodiversity, and more. (World Count) Food waste = water waste. As our food is thrown away and food production requires water, water is wasted at the same time. In the United States, food waste accounts for 25 percent of the country's water use. (World Count) About two-thirds of food waste at home is due to food not being used before it goes bad. Food spoilage at home occurs due to improper storage, lack of visibility in refrigerators, partially used ingredients and misjudged food needs. (FoodPrint) To help remedy the current situation, we developed Plater to help reduce food spoilage by giving you constant reminders of what you have in stock while providing you with a variety of stunning and easy-to-follow recipes.
